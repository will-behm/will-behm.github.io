My Work at Center For Coalfield Justice (CCJ)

Center for Coalfield Justice (CCJ) is an environmental justice nonprofit focused on advocacy, education, and organizing in Southwestern Pennsylvania. CCJ primarily operates in Washington County and Greene County, my home county. I first became interested in working on environmental issues when the land behind my home fell into the path of a natural gas pipeline. A fifty-foot wide and roughly quarter-mile long stretch of trees were clear cut, and I was left wondering about the human levers, decisions, and power structures involved in decimating a whole lot of life that was special to me. As such, CCJ was a logical first step in my career, where I could work on issues I knew about for people and a place that I care about!

Key Responsibilities

  • Photo Monitoring for Stream Subsidence in Ryerson State Park
  • Greene County Comprehensive Plan Review, Outreach, and public comment regarding fossil fuel developement
  • Assisting residents with identification and reporting of contamination, spills, and illegal waste management practices
