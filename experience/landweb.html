My Work at LandWEB, Inc.

LandWEB is nonprofit that provides biophysical landscape feedback to organic farms in the context of land management decisions made by their stewards. LandWEB's data system models the soil-plant-atmosphere continuum by combining in situ biomass characterization with remotely sensed measurments and 3D soil mapping. In my position, I've been fortunate enough to work alongside some long-time experts in the field of environmental modelling, and learned a ton about how they think! LandWEB takes great lengths to ensure data privacy for farms, in order to ensure that they maintain control over the ends that their data is used for. As such, I've left out my visualizations containing data that belongs to the farms - If I get permission or am able to scrub identity from some of my work, I'll post it here!

Key Responsibilities

  • Field and Geospatial Data Processing: Performing management, analysis, and QA/QC with Python.
  • Field Research: Monitoring a complex suite of biophysical variables, and operationalizing a Soil Mapping System.
  • Methods Development: Proposing and codifying protocol improvements from past field experience and meta-analysis.
  • Spatial Data Product Creation: Writing and implelemting workflows to curate individualized, symbolized data packages for farms that use multiple open source softwares.

Biomass Modeling

A large portion of my field duties have been regular data collection to build LandWEB's Biomass models. Using a set of traditional sampling equipment and modern sensors, some of which were built in-house, I worked with LandWEB's Field Science Lead to collect the suite of biophysical variables (including but not limited to GCI, PAR, LAI, and in situ). From roughly April to November, I spend ~3 days per week in the field, traveling to farms around the state.

Collecting in situ GCI, PAR, LAI, soil moisture, and more with LandWEB's custom-built "Grazibrater".

Soil Mapping

In the Summer of 2024, we operationalized LandWEB's soil mapping system. It consists of a DUALEM (an electromagnetic scanner towed behind a 4 wheeler), and a GeoProbe (truck mounted hydraulic probe) equipped with a penetrometer and 4-foot soil corer. Being a completely new set of equipment and protocols, we spent a lot of time troubleshooting in the field. Being such a nuanced set of instruments, it took a great display of scientific chops, collaboration, creative problem solving to get things working how we wanted! Further, I took an existing set of python scripts that applied correction and interpolation to the DUALEM data, made them ready for use by the field team, and built out the workflow to organize and prepare data for our visualization workflow, which I also designed.
